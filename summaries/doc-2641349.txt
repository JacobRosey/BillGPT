- **Bill Number**: H. R. 117
- **Introduced by**: Mr. Biggs (Representative)
- **Date Introduced**: January 9, 2023
- **Committees**: 
  - Oversight and Accountability
  - Energy and Commerce

- **Purpose of the Bill**:
  - To prevent any organization receiving Federal funds from COVID relief packages from requiring employees to get a COVID-19 vaccine.

- **Key Provisions**:
  - **Funding Restriction**: 
    - Any organization that receives funds from COVID relief cannot mandate COVID-19 vaccination for its employees.
  - **Return of Funds**: 
    - Organizations that violate this rule must return any funds received from the COVID relief package to the Federal Government.
  - **Definition of COVID Relief Package**:
    - Includes several specific laws that provided COVID-related funding, such as:
      - CARES Act
      - Coronavirus Preparedness and Response Supplemental Appropriations Act, 2020
      - Families First Coronavirus Response Act
      - Paycheck Protection Program and Health Care Enhancement Act
      - Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021
      - American Rescue Plan Act of 2021

- **Theoretical Context**:
  - This bill reflects ongoing debates around vaccination mandates, personal freedom, and the role of government in public health, particularly in the context of COVID-19. Discussions on vaccine mandates have been particularly prominent due to varied public reactions to health policies during the pandemic.
