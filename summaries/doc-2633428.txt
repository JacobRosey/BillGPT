- **Bill Title**: H. RES. 9 - Expressing the sense of the House of Representatives regarding China's responsibility for the COVID-19 pandemic
- **Date Introduced**: January 9, 2023
- **Introduced by**: Mr. Nehls
- **Referred to**: Committee on Foreign Affairs

- **Main Points**:
  - The resolution claims that:
    - COVID-19 originated in Wuhan, China, in December 2019.
    - The Chinese Communist Party (CCP) is accused of covering up the severity and existence of the virus.
    - There were efforts to destroy research and silence voices regarding the pandemic.
    - The CCP's actions allegedly allowed COVID-19 to spread worldwide by reporting false infection and death numbers.

- **Economic Impact**:
  - It details significant economic damage caused globally by the pandemic, including:
    - Estimated economic costs to the U.S. of $16 trillion.
    - A global drop in GDP by 3.9% from 2019 to 2020, noted as the worst economic downturn since the Great Depression.
    - High financial distress reported by households in major U.S. cities (New York City, Los Angeles, Chicago, Houston).
    - A significant percentage of Americans experienced job losses due to COVID-19.
    - Increase in extreme poverty globally associated with the pandemic.

- **Resolution Statements**:
  - Asserts that:
    - China is responsible for the COVID-19 pandemic.
    - The United States should be financially compensated by China for the pandemic's economic harm.

This resolution reflects ongoing tensions between the U.S. and China, particularly concerning the origin and handling of the pandemic, and indicates a call for accountability on an international scale.
